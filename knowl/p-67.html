One last important consideration about how we describe a function: we will consider two basic sorts of formulas: closed and recursive. A closed formula is one in which each output is given by an explicit rule based solely on its input. This is what most of us think of as a formula. For example, \(f(n) = 3n + 1\) is a closed formula, since to find \(f(5)\) (say) we take the input 5 and so something to it: multiply it by 5 and then add 1.
